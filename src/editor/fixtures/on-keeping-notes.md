# On keeping notes

A note is worth keeping when you can still open it in fifty years. That rules out most of what a notes app wants to sell you, and leaves something plain: a markdown file, in a folder you picked, that opens with `cat`.

Everything else is an editor sitting on top of the file. A good one gets out of the way: it sets a comfortable line length, shows the folder instead of promising privacy, and never manufactures urgency about your own writing.

## What the file remembers

- The text, exactly as you typed it
- Where it lives — `~/Notes/on-keeping-notes.md`
- Nothing you can't see by reading it

> Obiter is the least consequential thing in the stack. If it vanishes tomorrow, you lose an editor, not your work.
